When rating, adopt a highly supportive but pedagogically sound perspective. Match this specific annotator profile using the following rules:

1. **Reward Modeling for Complex/New Tasks**: Generous annotators LOVE when a tutor breaks down a complex word problem, sets up an equation, models the first step of long division, or uses visual tools. Do NOT penalize this as "preemptively giving the answer." For multi-step or difficult concepts, providing heavy initial modeling before letting the student take over is highly **Effective**.
2. **Penalize Over-scaffolding on Simple/Mastered Tasks**: You MUST downgrade to **Partial** or **Ineffective** if the tutor walks the student step-by-step through a concept they have already mastered (e.g., they just solved a similar problem), or if the tutor preemptively tells them how to solve a simple problem without checking their prior knowledge first. Let the student try first on things they should know.
3. **Judge by the Result (Confusion/Guessing = Failed Scaffold)**: A strategy is only Effective if it actually helps the student. If the tutor provides a hint, analogy, or push for rigor, but the student remains explicitly confused, resorts to random guessing, or the tutor just moves on without resolving the confusion, downgrade to **Partial** or **Ineffective**. Do not reward the mere "attempt" of a strategy if it fails.
4. **Penalize "Telling" without "Explaining"**: If the tutor simply gives the final answer, counts for the student, or points to the correct multiple-choice option without explaining *why* it's correct (or asking the student to explain why), downgrade to **Partial**.
5. **Forgive Minor Slips & Clumsy Explanations**: Generous annotators are extremely forgiving of tutor misstatements, flawed analogies, or confusing instructions, *provided* the tutor is actively trying to break down the problem and the student ultimately reaches understanding. Rate these as **Effective**. Do not use "Ineffective" just because a tutor's math logic was slightly clumsy.
6. **Accept the Scribe Role**: Do NOT penalize the tutor for taking over the writing, drawing, or mechanical interface clicking, as long as the student is engaged in the cognitive work.
7. **Allow Initial Pushes for Rigor**: It is **Effective** to push for rigor (e.g., asking "What do you think?") even when a student makes an error. But if the student is stuck, the tutor must follow up with support.
