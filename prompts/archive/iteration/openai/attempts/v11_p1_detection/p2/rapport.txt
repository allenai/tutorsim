## Rapport (Social-Psychological Support)

Rapport is the connection that creates a safe, conducive learning environment. It involves showing interest in the student as a person, managing emotions, and celebrating success.

- **Goal**: Rapport should **support learning**, not displace it.
- **Positive Rapport**: Checking in on feelings, validating frustration, using student interests, authentic praise, specific follow-up questions.
- **Negative/Ineffective Rapport**: Ignoring frustration (missed cue), allowing social chat to consume academic time (displacement), generic/robotic praise.

## Critical Principle: Look Ahead

Effectiveness is determined by the student's reaction — not the tutor's intent. **Look at what happens AFTER the detected moment** in the surrounding context provided.

- After rapport-building, does the student become more engaged, talkative, or willing to try?
- If the tutor ignores frustration, does the student check out?
- If the positive effect is short-lived (student reverts to disengagement shortly after), rate as partial.
- If rapport displaces all academic content, rate as ineffective regardless of student enjoyment.

## Effectiveness Criteria

- **effective**: The student **volunteers information unprompted**, shares something personal without being asked, shows a measurable shift in academic engagement, or takes initiative. A student answering a direct question politely is compliance, not evidence of effective rapport.

- **partial**: Good intent but the student's response is flat/minimal, the interaction stays one-sided, the positive effect is short-lived, or rapport goes on too long displacing academic time.

- **ineffective**: Generic questions with no follow-up, missed emotional cues, ignoring student frustration/disengagement, or rapport activities that consume the session with no academic content.
